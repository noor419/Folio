Manufacturing Project

 

The model CVT transmission is a collaborative project completed with a team of six students. Use of SolidWorks and HSMWorks allowed for the manufacturing of some parts of the model.

Details are outlined below.

Date:  Feb. 2019 - May 2019
Skillsets:  Engineering Design & Manufacturing

Tools Used:
      • Drill Press
      • Lathe
      • CNC Mill
      • Band Saw

 


 

 

 

The CVT transmission is based off the infinite gear system that usually exists in automobiles today.
For our purposes, a simple model using two cones and a belt loop around them, was utilized.

 

 

 

Materials List 

 

○   plastic ball bearings (x4)
○   3" diameter acetal rod
○   guide rail for track roller
○   track roller
○   0.5" wide flat belt
○   stainless steel ball bearings (x4)
○   0.25" diameter steel rod
○   steel corner brackets (x4)
○   steel binding barrels and screws
○   d-profile rotary shaft
○   plexiglass or plastic sheets

 

 

 

Procedure

 

  1. First, the measurements and materials were finalized for the model.

  2. For certain parts made of plexiglass or plastic to be manufactured, it was necessary to model them using Solidworks. These parts included the housing and gears.

  3. After modeling the parts and creating the G-code using HSMWorks, they were manufactured using the CNC mill.

  4. The acetal rod was cut in half by a band saw, which was then shaped into two cones by the lathe.

  5. The base and walls were also cut with a bandsaw out of plexiglass.

  6. Holes for the screws and bearings were drilled using a drill press.

  7. After the parts were completely manufactured, everything was assembled by hand together.

  8. Finally, after testing and validation, the CVT transmission model was complete.
