DIY Project

 

The 10" coffee table is a project that introduces the basics of metalworking. Once the materials were selected for the design, a variety of tools were used to complete the product.

The full procedure is outlined below.

Date:  August 2019
Skillsets:  Metal Fabrication & Manufacturing

Tools Used:
      • Drill Press
      • Angle Grinder
      • Metal Bender
      • MIG Welding Machine

 


 

 

 

Procedure

 

  1. First, the proper materials were gathered. This project comprised of a thin steel metal sheet, steel tubing rods, wood screws, and a 12"x12" wood board.

  2. The next step was measuring out the materials to the proper sizes. 2 steel pieces were measured on the sheet to 11" as the width was already at 1.5". Two thin metal rods were measured out to 36". Consequent markings had to made at 13" and 10" from the end, as these will be the locations where the metal will be bent.

  3. Using an angle grinder, the two 1.5"x11" steel sheets and the two 36" metal rods need to be cut.

  4. Once the metal is cut to size, the rods have to be bent. Using the metal bender, the two rods are to be bent at the 13" and 10" markings to form a U shape for the table legs.

  5. The steel sheets need to be marked with the location of the proposed wood screws. Once these holes are marked, they are to be drilled with a drill press.

  6. After bending the rods and drilling the holes on the sheets, the two items must be welded together to make a pair of table legs to be attached to the wood tabletop.

  7. Once the legs are completed, the wood piece is to be sanded and oiled. You also want to verify and make sure that the legs are straight and are at the same height.

  8. The final step is to drill the top face of the legs into the tabletop using wood screws and a power drill. Once that step is completed, make sure the table sits straight on a flat surface without wobbling. Your coffee table is now complete.
