DIY Project

 

The DIY soap dish project is an at-home assignment to turn materials found at home into usable products. Using spare bamboo chopsticks, a soap dish was fabricated to hold small items.

The full procedure is outlined below.

Date:  May 2020
Skillsets:  Product Design & Manufacturing

Tools Used:
      • Power Drill
      • Sewing Needle
      • Hot Glue Gun
      • X-ACTO Knife / Cutting Blade

 


 

 

 

The first step was to come up with the design. The design was taken from inspiration found online, as seen below. I dissected the parts and deducted that half of the chopsticks that I would need would be grouped together in a fanned out manner. This would create two pieces of the dish that would interlace and come together at the center to create the body. The materials I had on hand were hot glue and string, so I used those and incorporated them into my design. I decided I'd use the hot glue as my main source of keeping the pieces together, and that I would thread the string at the center between each chopstick for added support.

 

Procedure

 

  1. Gather the required materials. Have standard wooden chopsticks at hand, some hot glue, beads for embellishment, and some thin string. You will need at least 12 chopsticks.

  2. The chopsticks used in this project had a length of 9.5 inches. In order to make the dish of a more substantial size, 2.5 inches was cut off at the thicker end of each stick using a cutting blade. You can also sand off the cut face which is optional for a smoother finish.

  3. The next step is separating all 12 chopsticks into two sets of 6. The chopsticks need to be fanned out in the way shown at the right, with the thinner ends touching each other. Holding the sets in place, interlace them together and adjust the height of the dish according to what you want. You want to mark off where the chopsticks all come together at the center. Take each stick and drill a small hole with a power drill at their center marks. Once the drilling is complete, lay the sets out flat with the centers lined up. Take your sewing needle with thin string and thread through each stick tightly while keeping everything secure.

  4. The final step is taking the threaded sticks and folding them upward on either side and hot gluing them at the ends encircled in the sketches. The center can also be hot glued for added support. To finish off, you can add bead embellishments wherever you'd like.
